Elderly People

Elderly people, also known as seniors or older adults, typically refer to individuals who are 65 years of age or older. As people age, they may experience various physical and cognitive changes, which can impact their mobility, independence, and overall well-being. Some common issues faced by elderly people include chronic illnesses, such as heart disease, diabetes, and arthritis, cognitive decline, mobility issues, falls, and social isolation. However, there are many resources available to support the aging population, such as healthcare services, housing options, and community programs that promote social connection and engagement. By providing adequate support and care for the elderly population, we can help to ensure that they can age with dignity and maintain a good quality of life.

Challenges They Face

  • Ageism and discrimination
  • Physical health concerns, such as chronic illnesses, mobility issues, and falls
  • Cognitive decline, such as dementia and Alzheimer's disease
  • Financial insecurity
  • Social isolation and loneliness
  • Caregiving and support needs
  • Elder abuse and neglect
  • Bereavement and loss
  • Housing and transportation issues
  • Access to healthcare and medical support

KUZA FOUNDATION INITIATIVE TO HELP THE ELDERLY IN THE SOCIETY

  • Healthcare services: Kuza provide healthcare services that are specifically tailored to the needs of elderly individuals, such as geriatric care, rehabilitation support, and mental health services.
  • Community programs: Kuza develop and implement community programs that promote social connection, engagement, and active aging. This can include senior centers, volunteer opportunities, and cultural events.
  • Advocacy and policy development: Kuza advocate for the rights and interests of elderly individuals, lobby for policy changes that benefit seniors, and work to ensure that existing policies are implemented effectively.
  • Caregiving support: Kuza provide support and resources to family caregivers, such as respite care, support groups, and educational resources.
  • Financial assistance: Kuza provide financial assistance to elderly individuals who are struggling to meet their basic needs, such as food and housing.
